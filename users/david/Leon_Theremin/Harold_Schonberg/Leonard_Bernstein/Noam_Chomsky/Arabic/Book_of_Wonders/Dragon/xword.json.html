The codex was probably bound during the reign of Jalayirid Sultan Ahmad (1382-1410) at Baghdad, and includes texts on astronomy, astrology, geomancy and a section of full-page illustrated plates dedicated to each discourse topic, e.g. a folktale, a sign of the zodiac, a prophet, etc.\n\n\n== History of Manuscript ==\nThe calligrapher, copiest and compiler, \u02bfAbd al-Hasan ibn Ahmad ibn \u02bfAli ibn al-Hasan al-Isfahani, whose family came from Isfahan in Iran and himself was a native of Baghdad, where he studied the Aristotelic \u2018demonstrative\u2019 (burhan) sciences. It seems the Kitab al-Bulhan was commissioned by \u2013 or the idea of \u2013 Shaykh al-Diya Husayn al-Irbili\u2019 (originally of Irbil near Mosul in northern Iraq), who sold it to Haydar ibn al-Hajji \u02bfAbd al-Karim ibn Muhammad in Dec 1409 - Jan 1410. The original codex comprised a series of treatises, which came apart, and when sections were reassembled and some lost, it became jumbled and incoherent. The work includes extracts copied from the Kitab al-mawalid (\u2018Book of Nativities\u2019) of the astronomer and neo-Platonist Abu Ma\u02bfshar al-Balkhi (787-886 CE) of Balkh (modern-day Mazar-i Sharif) in northern Afghanistan.\n\n\n== Manuscript copies ==\nIn the late 16th century, two Turkish copies were made from the original for the two daughters of the Ottoman sultan Murad III: Ay\u015fe Sultan (c. 1582) and Fatma Sultan. These manuscripts are complete and establish the original order of the treatises of the Kitab al-Bulhan.
